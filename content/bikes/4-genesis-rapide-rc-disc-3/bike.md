Title: Genesis Rapide RC Disc 3

----

Text: 

Turns out the Rapide sub-brand from Genesis/Madison bikes was short-lived; it's no longer around, with all social media and website presence gone in the blink of any eye.

Shame, as the folks over at Stereo did a (link: https://stereocreative.com/work/rapide text: great job on the branding). 

As a result I got an amazing deal on this frame on a cycling forum. For a carbon fibre, disc-based frameset for the price I did, it was daylight robbery. And quickly became my next project. I sold my trusty Felt Z-95 (clocking 11k+ km) and focused on getting the various parts for this, what is my first ever carbon fibre bike. 

TL;DR: Carbon fibre is plastic and over-rated, at least based on my experience so far. I also went through some serious (link: https://www.britishcycling.org.uk/knowledge/training/health-recovery/article/izn20140820-Physiotherapy-Ask-the-Experts--Hand-and-wrist-pain-on-the-bike-0 text: ulnar nerve pain) that seems to have been solved by swapping out the 90mm Bontrager stem with a parts bin 100mm job.

The other major blocker in parts was the wheelset. Thankfully since upgrading to Mason x Hunts on my Ribble, I had the Mavic All Roads left over, with the added bonus that they're as interchangeable as Hope for axle sizes and standards.
