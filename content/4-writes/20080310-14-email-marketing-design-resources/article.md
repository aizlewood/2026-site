----

Date: 2008-03-10

Text: 

Like it or not, HTML email marketing campaigns are a core requirement for any successful email marketing program.  Not only do they offer the first rule for successful online marketing – metrics – but they also offer a host of benefits that you simply can’t get from using text-only email messages.  Things like brand loyalty and identity reinforcement, better product placement and promotion, and increased flexibility within the email to name a few!  Of course, HTML campaigns will always have its detractors, and this audience have their own valid reasons not to condone HTML email, but in the interest of the advantages listed above, this format of email is unquestionably crucial in today’s multi-channel, multi-brand, and highly competitive marketing environment!

So, here’s a list to help all email marketers design the ultimate email.  You’ll find information, inspiration, downloads, tools (free &amp; paid) and further reading.  Of course I haven’t selected everything out there or this post would be a mile long, but if you feel I’ve missed any important ones, feel free to leave a comment.

<h3>Free Tools:</h3>
<a href="http://code.dunae.ca/premailer.web/"><img class="alignleft size-medium wp-image-161" title="premailer" src="http://www.carbongraffiti.com/beta/wp-content/uploads/2008/04/premailer.gif" alt="Dunae" width="200" height="100" /></a>It’s one thing designing a great-looking email, but will it work in all major clients? Another Canadian representative, Alex from Dunae has designed ‘PreMailer’, a tool for analyzing the CSS you use in your finished email campaign, and offering suggestions on how that CSS will perform in all major email clients.  Pure genius. Check it out over at <a title="premailer" href="http://code.dunae.ca/premailer.web/">http://code.dunae.ca/premailer.web/</a>

<a href="http://litmusapp.com/"><img class="alignleft size-medium wp-image-162" title="litmus" src="http://www.carbongraffiti.com/beta/wp-content/uploads/2008/04/litmus.gif" alt="LitmusApp" width="200" height="100" /></a>A semi-free tool, these guys used to be SiteVista and rebranded to LitmusApp recently.  Whereas PreMailer gives you text-based suggestions, this tool from LitmusApp gives you full visualizations on how your email looks in all major clients.  Though not currently offering all major clients, they will be soon enough.  This tool is available for a 1 week free trial.  Find it at <a title="LitmusApp" href="http://litmusapp.com/">http://litmusapp.com/</a>

<h3>Paid Tools:</h3>
<a href="http://www.returnpath.net/senderscore/"><img class="alignleft size-medium wp-image-163" title="sender" src="http://www.carbongraffiti.com/beta/wp-content/uploads/2008/04/sender.gif" alt="SenderScore" width="200" height="100" /></a>Unfortunately the big tools aren’t free.  I’ve used it in the past, and ReturnPath’s SenderScore is a great integrated tool that not only shows you visual reps of your email (across all clients) it also tells you your Spam score based on a continually updated set of rules and filters.  What’s more, this app also offers reputation and deliverability monitoring.  You definitely get what you pay for with ReturnPath. <a title="ReturnPath SenderScore" href="http://www.returnpath.net/senderscore/"> http://www.returnpath.net/senderscore/ </a>

<a href="http://www.mailchimp.com/add-ons/inboxinspector/"><img class="alignleft size-medium wp-image-164" title="chimp3" src="http://www.carbongraffiti.com/beta/wp-content/uploads/2008/04/chimp3.gif" alt="MailChimp" width="200" height="100" /></a>As mentioned earlier, MailChimp are also getting in on the whole ‘test it before you send it’ act.  Their InboxInspector is a standalone tool that you don’t have to buy a MailChimp account to use – you can simply create a free account, sign up for the II, and keep using your existing ESP.  Find them at <a title="InboxInspector" href="http://www.mailchimp.com/add-ons/inboxinspector/">http://www.mailchimp.com/add-ons/inboxinspector/ </a>

<h3>Knowledge:</h3>n<a href="http://www.email-standards.org/"><img class="alignleft size-medium wp-image-165" title="esp" src="http://www.carbongraffiti.com/beta/wp-content/uploads/2008/04/esp.gif" alt="ESP" width="200" height="100" /></a>Finally, the ultimate example of ‘catch a man a fish’ and all that, and as I’ve posted about in the past, we have the ultimate in Email Design Holy Grails in the form of the much applauded Email Standards Project at <a title="ESP" href="http://www.email-standards.org/">http://www.email-standards.org/</a>.  These guys have taken the email design issue by the scruff of the neck and are actually getting the big guys – Yahoo, Gmail, Outlook (hopefully) to take notice of the need for Email standards compliance.  We have it on the web (kind of, cheers IE6), but we need it for email too.
