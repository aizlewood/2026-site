Title: Design the Perfect Email: Bonus Tips

----

Date: 2008-03-05

----

Text: 

Some quick bonus tips on designing the perfect email.

<ul>
  <li><em><strong>ALWAYS TEST</strong></em> on every major email client and web browser (for online versions) for links, copy, spelling, design, personalization, and SPAM score.</li>
  <li>Consider using <strong>short, teaser copy</strong> for each main article. The <strong>Cliffhanger-Effect </strong>(or Zeigarnik-Effect) shows that consumers feel the need to answer unanswered questions, articles or problems, and that they’re more likely to click-through to an article when prompted with an opener only.</li>
  <li><strong>Segment your data</strong>. The more segmented your data, the more personalized it becomes. Metrics will increase noticeably as recipients respond to relevance to them and their needs.</li>
  <li><strong>Never send similar content</strong> emails to same recipients within 2 weeks of each other. Otherwise you risk large unsub rates or worse (ISP complaints).</li>
  <li><strong>If sending similar content</strong> to same recipients as part of a touch/conversion process, ensure your content is clear, and obvious that it is Step X of a X step process.</li>
  <li>While text links for calls-to-action help with SPAM filters, <strong>large clickable image buttons</strong> get higher clicks.</li>
  <li>Try to <strong>place your average recipient</strong> into 2 main groups:  B-to-B (business to business) and B-to-C (business to consumer).

    <ul>
    <li><strong>B-to-B</strong>:  These users primarily use desktop-based email clients administered by a dedicated IT group, which includes spam filter rules. Common B-to-B email clients include Outlook and Lotus Notes.  It is worth noting that Outlook should be divided into 2 sub-groups: Pre-Outlook 2007 and Post-Outlook 2007.</li>
    <li><strong>General rule-of-thumb</strong>: Table-based mark-up using inline CSS.</li>
    <li><strong>B-to-C</strong>:  These users are a mixed bag, using both desktop-based email clients that include Thunderbird and Eudora, as well as web-based email clients including Yahoo, Hotmail/Live, Gmail, and .Mac.</li>
    <li><strong>General rule-of-thumb</strong>: Either CSS or table-based mark-up, with diligent testing.</li>
  </ul>
</li>
</ul>
